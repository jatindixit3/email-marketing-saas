## What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, smartphone, tablet) when you visit a website. Cookies allow websites to:

- Remember your preferences and settings
- Provide personalized content and recommendations
- Analyze how you use the website
- Improve website performance and user experience

## Managing Your Cookie Preferences

### Browser Settings

You can control cookies through your browser settings:

**Google Chrome:**
1. Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
2. Choose: Block all cookies / Block third-party cookies / Allow all cookies

**Mozilla Firefox:**
1. Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
2. Choose: Standard / Strict / Custom

**Safari:**
1. Preferences → Privacy
2. Check: Block all cookies / Prevent cross-site tracking

**Microsoft Edge:**
1. Settings → Privacy, search, and services → Cookies and site permissions
2. Choose: Block all cookies / Block third-party cookies / Allow all cookies

**Note:** Blocking essential cookies may prevent you from using core features of the Service.

## Cookie Lifespan

Cookies have different lifespans:

### Session Cookies

- Deleted when you close your browser
- Used for: Authentication, temporary preferences

### Persistent Cookies

- Remain on your device for a specified period
- Used for: Long-term preferences, analytics
- Duration: From 24 hours to 2 years (as specified in tables above)

You can delete persistent cookies at any time through your browser settings.
